What is the Internet of Things?

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of interconnected devices that have software, internet connectivity, and are embedded with censors. IoTs use their internet connection to collect and transfer data without needing a humans help with getting input. A helpful website article with an embedded video explaing IoTs in more detail is shown on builtin.com

Appliances, vehicles, and wearable devices will then communicate with us or amongst themselves to collect and share data, allowing these devices to become more interactive and efficient. Please refer to builtin.com again for 30 examples of IoT devices.
